Drake

In 1986, Aubrey Drake Graham was born in Toronto, Canada.

Drake grew up with music all around him. His father was a drummer and his uncle was a bassist for a famous singer.

He had two very diverse parents--his mother was a white Canadian jew, and his father was an african-american catholic.

At the age of 5, his parents divorced, and Drake was raised by his mother in Forest Hill, a neighborhood in Toronto. Drake said he felt different from everyone else in his all-white school.

One of Drake’s classmates had a father who was an agent, and Drake auditioned for the agent. He landed a role on the teen drama Degrassi: The Next Generation.

Drake played the role of “wheelchair Jimmy,” a high school basketball star who was paralyzed after getting shot.

In 2006, while still acting on Degrassi, Drake released his first mixtape Room for Improvement which had 6,000 sales.

The next year, he released his second mixtape Comeback Season with Drake’s first hit single “Replacement Girl.” Artist Jas Prince was so impressed by the song that he played it for Lil Wayne.

In 2008, Drake received a surprise call from Lil Wayne asking Drake to join him on tour. Throughout the tour, Lil Wayne and Drake recorded several songs.

After the tour, Drake released his third mixtape, So Far Gone. The mixtape had 2,000 downloads in two hours. His hit song, “Best I Ever Had” peaked at #2 on the Billboard Top 100.

Since then, Drake has released several albums and mixtapes, winning him multiple Grammy Awards and MTV Video Music Awards.
