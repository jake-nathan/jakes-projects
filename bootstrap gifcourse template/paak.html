Anderson.Paak

Anderson Paak was born in Oxnard, California, where he began producing music in his bedroom as a teenager.

He got his first experience performing as a drummer at his local church where he met his first wife. They divorced after a few years.

He began teaching at a music school when he met his second wife, a music student from South Korea.

Paak also worked at a marijuana farm in Santa Barbara. He was laid off without warning, making him homeless with his wife and son to take care of.

In 2011, he moved to Los Angeles where he started making music under the name “Breezy Lovejoy.” He was also the drummer for an American Idol contestant.

His big breakthrough came when he performed 6 songs on Dr. Dre’s third album, Compton. He also appeared on other albums such as Schoolboy Q’s Blank Face LP.

In 2016, Paak released his second album, Malibu, which received critical acclaim. He still has a close relationship with Dr. Dre.

Paak earned a spot on XXL’s prestigious Freshman Class, alongside rappers like Lil Uzi Vert, Kodack Black, and Lil Dicky.

In 2017, J. Cole announced that Anderson .Paak would be joining him on his tour.
