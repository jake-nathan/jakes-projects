Ellen DeGeneres

Ellen grew up outside New Orleans and lived there until she 13 years old. She said she use to love to bike around the city as a child.

When she was 13, Ellen’s parents divorced. She used comedy to help cheer up her mom. “She’d laugh so hard she’d start to cry” - Ellen

In 1982, Ellen won a national talent contest earning the title of "Funniest Person in America.” This put her on the national scene.

In 1986, Ellen appeared on the Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson. She was the first female comic to be interviewed after her stand-up routine.

Eventually, Degeneres had her own sitcom called Ellen, which received multiple Emmy Award nominations and a Peabody Award.

Ellen made history when she came out as gay on an episode of her show titled, “The Puppy Episode.” The controversial episode garnered 46 million views.

After the episode, Ellen faced harsh criticisms for focusing too much on the sexuality of her character. Ultimately, the show was cancelled in 1998.

After the cancelling of the show, Ellen was discouraged, saying she went through a phase where she thought “Everyone hates me now.”

In 2003, Ellen was the voice of Dory in the highest-grossing animated film of all time, Finding Nemo. She received high praise for her performance.

After Finding Nemo, Ellen found herself hosting a daytime talk show called Ellen. The show received a record-breaking 12 Emmy nominations in year 1.

Ellen continues to host the show, bringing on a variety of candidates from rapper Kanye West to funny little kids.

Ellen took an iconic selfie at the Oscars that was retweeted 750,000 times in 45 minutes!

Ellen continues to be an activist for several movements, including the ethical treatment of animals and AIDS. Her efforts earned her the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

We love you, Ellen!
