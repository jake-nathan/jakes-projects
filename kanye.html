Kanye West

Kanye was born in Atlanta, Georgia. His parents divorced when he was 3 years old, so he moved to Chicago with his mother.

West loved the arts from an early age, writing poems at the age of 5. He started rapping in 3rd grade and started composing music in 7th grade.

West’s mother, Donda, was always a huge supporter of Kanye. She paid for recording sessions at studios for Kanye to pursue his musical passions.

West developed a close relationship with producer No I.D., who became Kanye’s mentor. He taught Kanye how to sample and create beats.

When he was 20, Kanye dropped out of college to work on his musical career full-time. He started making beats for local artists.

Kanye rose to prominence when he produced several songs on Jay Z’s popular album The Blueprint. He also produced songs for several other high-profile artists.

Although he was great at producing, Kanye’s heart was in rapping. After several record labels turned him down, he finally signed a deal with Capitol Records.

On October 23, 2002, Kanye fell asleep at the wheel and was involved in a car crash, shattering his jaw. He had to have his jaw wired shut.

His car crash experience inspired him to record the song “Through The Wire.” Kanye rapped in the song while his jaw was still wired shut.

In 2004, Kanye dropped his first album, The College Dropout. The album went triple platinum and earned Kanye a Grammy award for Best Rap Album of the Year.

Since then, Kanye has released many more albums and singles, earning him 21 Grammy awards and 100 million digital song downloads. He is one of the most decorated artists of all time.
