Elon Musk

Elon Musk was born in South Africa. When he was just 10 years old, he developed a love for computers.

During his childhood, Musk taught himself how to program. At 12, he made is his first software sale--it was a game he programmed called Blastar.

When he was 17, he moved to Canada for college, and ended up graduating from UPenn in the United States. For graduate school, he attended Stanford.

After 2 days at Stanford, he decided to drop out to start his own company, Zip2 corporation.

In 1999, he sold Zip2 for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options to a division of the Compaq Computer Corporation.

In that year, he also founded X.com, a financial services company. That company went on to become PayPal, which was acquired by eBay for $1.5 billion.

Elon had always been obsessed with space exploration, so in 2002, he founded SpaceX. By 2008, the company delivered supplies to the international space station.
